About Cavite

The Historical Capital of the Philippines — where revolution, culture, and natural beauty converge.

Location & Background

Cavite is a province in the CALABARZON region (Region IV-A) in the Philippines, located on the southwestern shore of Luzon island, just south of Metro Manila. It is bordered by Manila Bay to the north, Laguna to the east, Batangas to the south, and the province of Rizal to the northeast.

The province covers approximately 1,575 km² and is composed of 8 cities and 23 municipalities, with Trece Martires City as its capital. With a population of over 4 million, it is one of the most densely populated provinces outside Metro Manila.

Strategically positioned as a gateway between Metro Manila and southern Luzon, Cavite is a major economic hub boasting a blend of modern commerce, industrial zones, and rich natural and cultural heritage.

A Short History

Cavite is famously known as the "Historical Capital of the Philippines." It played a central role in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule in the late 19th century. Generals Emilio Aguinaldo and Andres Bonifacio both had deep roots in this province.

In June 1898, the Philippine Declaration of Independence was proclaimed in Kawit, Cavite — making it one of the most historically significant locations in Southeast Asia. The Aguinaldo Shrine in Kawit marks this momentous event.

During the American colonial period and World War II, Cavite continued to serve as a strategic military point, with Corregidor Island standing as a symbol of Filipino-American resistance against Japanese forces.

Culture & Traditions

The people of Cavite, known as Caviteños, have a rich blend of indigenous Filipino traditions infused with Spanish colonial influences that span over three centuries. The Caviteño dialect — a creole language mixing Spanish and Filipino — is unique to Cavite City.

Festivals are central to Caviteño culture. The Regada Festival in Kawit celebrates the proclamation of independence with water festivities, while the Pahimis Festival in Amadeo honors the coffee harvest with cultural shows and trade fairs.

Religious traditions remain strong, with centuries-old processions, the Senakulo (Passion play) during Holy Week, and patron-saint fiestas celebrated in every municipality.
