Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common visitor questions.

By bus, take DLTB, HM Transport, or Genesis Bus from the Buendia Terminal (EDSA–Taft) bound for Tagaytay or other Cavite destinations. By car, use the Cavite Expressway (CAVITEX) or Aguinaldo Highway. Ride-hailing apps like Grab are also available from most Cavite cities.

The dry season from November to May is ideal. Tagaytay is particularly lovely from December to February when cool highland air is at its most refreshing. Avoid June–October for trekking and island travel, though waterfalls are at their fullest after the rains.

Visits depend on PHIVOLCS (Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology) advisories. Always check the latest alert level before planning a trip to the island. When it is open, boat tours depart from Talisay town and guided hikes are available. Contact PHIVOLCS at (02) 8929-9254 for the latest updates.

Yes! Local tour operators offer day-tour and overnight packages covering Tagaytay, Kawit heritage sites, Corregidor Island, mountain treks, and coffee farm visits. Fill out the inquiry form above and select "Guided Tours" to request more information.

Top pasalubong picks include: Kapeng Barako (ground or whole bean) from Amadeo, sineguelas jam from Tagaytay markets, sampalok candy from Cavite City, bibingka espesyal from roadside stalls, and dried tawilis from Mahogany Market.

A comfortable day trip to Tagaytay typically costs ₱800–₱2,000 per person, covering transport, meals (bulalo + tawilis), entrance fees, and pasalubong. Historical sites like the Aguinaldo Shrine charge very affordable entrance fees of ₱30–₱200. Weekday visits are less crowded and often cheaper.
