Festivals & Culture

Celebrate the vibrant traditions, colorful festivals, and rich cultural heritage that define the Caviteño spirit.

Our Celebrated Festivals

Cavite's festivals are immersive, community-driven celebrations that bring history and heritage to life.

Regada Water Festival

📍 Kawit 📅 June

The Regada Festival is Kawit's grandest celebration, held every June 12 to mark Philippine Independence Day. Revelers drench each other with water in a joyful reenactment of the revolutionary celebration. Streets are closed, street dances are performed, and the town erupts in festivities that honor the declaration of independence proclaimed in Kawit in 1898. It is one of the most exuberant festivals in the entire CALABARZON region.

Pahimis Coffee Festival

📍 Amadeo 📅 January

The Pahimis Festival celebrates the coffee harvest season in Amadeo, the "Coffee Capital of Cavite." The word "pahimis" means the harvesting of coffee beans. The festival features a grand street dance parade, coffee exhibits and trade fair, cooking competitions, cultural shows, and the crowning of the festival queen. It highlights Amadeo's world-class Barako coffee and its role as a leading coffee-producing municipality in the Philippines.

Salubungan Festival

📍 General Trias 📅 March

The Salubungan Festival of General Trias commemorates the historic meeting (salubungan) between General Emilio Aguinaldo's forces and the revolutionaries during the Philippine Revolution. Celebrated in March, the festival features a grand parade, cultural performances, street dancing competitions in traditional revolutionary costumes, and historical reenactments that bring the province's revolutionary past to vivid life.

Binhi Festival

📍 Silang 📅 April

The Binhi (Seed) Festival of Silang celebrates the municipality's agricultural heritage and the spirit of growth and renewal. "Binhi" symbolizes both the literal seeds planted by Silang's farmers and the seeds of progress sown by its community. The festival includes flower and garden exhibits, farm produce shows, street parades, cultural dances, and a trade fair showcasing Silang's agri-tourism products and garden restaurants.

Tagaytay City Fiesta

📍 Tagaytay 📅 May

The Tagaytay City Fiesta is held annually in May in honor of the city's patron saint. The celebration spans several days and includes a grand procession, religious rites, cultural performances, food festivals showcasing bulalo and tawilis, and entertainment events. The fiesta is also a major commercial event that draws thousands of visitors to sample Tagaytay's famous cuisine and enjoy the highland scenery at its most festive.

Kawit Heritage Festival

📍 Kawit 📅 November

The Kawit Heritage Festival is an annual celebration of the town's profound historical legacy. The festival includes cultural walks through ancestral homes and heritage sites, heritage exhibits, traditional Caviteño music and dance performances, an ancestral costume parade, and lectures on local history. It is a deeply educational and culturally immersive event that honors the heroes and heritage of Kawit — the birthplace of Philippine independence.

Caviteño Cultural Identity

Beyond festivals, Cavite's culture is woven into everyday life.

🗣️

Caviteño Language

The Caviteño creole dialect blends Spanish and Filipino, spoken uniquely in Cavite City — a living relic of over 300 years of colonial history.

⛪

Heritage Churches

Centuries-old Spanish baroque churches dot every municipality, from the Imus Cathedral to the Maragondon Church, holding active masses and processions.

🎶

Traditional Music

Kundiman and harana (serenade) traditions remain alive in Cavite, especially during town fiestas and courtship celebrations.

🍃

Herbal Healing

Traditional hilot healing and herbal medicine practices are preserved and practiced in Cavite's rural communities and wellness villages.
