Travel Tips

Everything you need to know before visiting Cavite — from the best time to go to staying safe on the road.

Plan Your Trip Wisely

Use these tips to get the most out of your Cavite adventure.

🌤️

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Cavite is during the dry season from November to May. The highlands of Tagaytay are most comfortable in December to February when the cool air is at its crispest. Avoid the rainy season (June–October) for trekking and island trips, though waterfalls are at their most impressive after rains.

🚌

Getting There & Around

  • From Manila: Take DLTB, HM Transport, or Genesis Bus bound for Tagaytay at Buendia Terminal
  • By car: Cavite Expressway (CAVITEX) or Aguinaldo Highway from Manila
  • Local transport: Jeepneys, tricycles, and UV Express vans connect municipalities
  • Tagaytay: Jeepneys from Nuvali or from Olivarez (Dasmariñas)
  • Ride-hailing: Grab is available in most Cavite cities
🔒

Safety Reminders

  • Keep valuables secure and avoid displaying expensive items in crowds
  • Register with the local barangay before trekking mountain trails
  • Always hire a registered local guide for mountain hikes
  • Monitor PHIVOLCS advisories before visiting Taal Volcano Island
  • Swim only at designated areas; observe swimming advisories
  • Inform someone of your itinerary when going on remote trails
🍽️

Food & Dining Tips

  • Try bulalo and tawilis at the Mahogany Market in Tagaytay for the best prices
  • Visit Gourmet Farms in Silang for a unique farm-to-table experience
  • Bring cash — many roadside stalls and wet markets don't accept cards
  • Barako coffee is best enjoyed black or with muscovado sugar, not creamer
  • Stock up on pasalubong (sineguelas jam, sampalok candy, dried tawilis) before leaving
🎒

What to Pack

  • Tagaytay / highlands: Light jacket or sweater — temperature can drop to 15°C at night
  • Trekking: Sturdy closed-toe shoes, sun protection, 2L+ water
  • Beach: Rash guard, reef-safe sunscreen, waterproof bag
  • General: Insect repellent, first aid kit, portable charger
  • Rain jacket or umbrella during the wet season (June–October)
🌿

Eco-Travel & Responsible Tourism

  • Pack out all your trash — leave no trace on trails and beaches
  • Use reusable bags and water bottles to reduce plastic waste
  • Support local vendors instead of large commercial chains
  • Respect wildlife and do not disturb natural habitats
  • Observe "no entry" zones near active volcanic areas
  • Follow local environmental ordinances regarding fires and noise
📞

💳

Budget & Money Tips

  • Tagaytay is more expensive than other Cavite towns — budget ₱800–₱2,000 per person per day
  • ATMs are widely available in Tagaytay, Imus, and Dasmariñas City
  • Smaller municipalities have limited ATMs — withdraw cash in advance
  • Entrance fees for most historical sites are very affordable (₱30–₱200)
  • Weekday visits are cheaper and less crowded than weekends
